Angular Momentum Transport in Molecular Clouds

My work explores how angular momentum is acquired, redistributed, and transported across scales during cloud collapse and star formation.

I am particularly interested in the role of filamentary flows, turbulent motions, gravity, and magnetic fields in shaping the angular momentum budgets of dense gas and forming objects.

Active Torques in Molecular Clouds

This line of work studies the torques that act within molecular-cloud structures and how they contribute to angular momentum redistribution.

I examine the relative roles of hydrodynamic, gravitational, magnetic, and pressure-related torques, and how their balance depends on geometry, environment, and evolutionary stage.

Angular Momentum Transport in Filaments

I study whether gravity-driven longitudinal flows along filaments can channel and reorient angular momentum toward embedded collapse centers.

This connects filament kinematics, convergent motions, and the possible emergence of preferred outflow–filament alignments during later stages of evolution.

Synthetic Observations

I use synthetic observations to connect numerical simulations with observable diagnostics, including dust continuum and molecular-line tracers.

These tools help bridge theoretical predictions with observed structures and kinematics in star-forming environments.
